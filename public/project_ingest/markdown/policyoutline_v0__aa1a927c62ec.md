DECISION 1: While we acknowledge potential benefits from AI applications in the school system:

We see a lesser and less immediate need to develop policy to accelerate and promote GenAI adoption in the schools.

We see a greater and more immediate need to develop policy to protect learning by restricting certain GenAI applications in the schools.

Pragmatic Constraints on Restriction of GenAI Applications

We acknowledge limits on our ability to fully enforce GenAI restrictions when work is un-proctored

We pursue opportunities to move summative assessments into a proctored environment, but acknowledge constraints.

We value maintaining positive and trusting rapport between students and teachers

We are motivated to protect students from patently unfair outcomes:

False accusations of cheating

Competing with peers who are in rampant non-compliance with GenAI restrictions

DECISION 2: So we steer between two extremes:

Abandoning our idealized goals due to pragmatic constraints leads to race-to-the-bottom conditions. Students lose the freedom not to use GenAI.

Pursuing our idealized restrictions, at all costs, creates a climate of mistrust and fear that is its own impediment to learning
